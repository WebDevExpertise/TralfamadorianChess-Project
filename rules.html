Every fifteen moves, the flow of the game shifts dramatically as one player is granted two consecutive moves; this leaves their opponent momentarily powerless without choice. The player who receives this advantage is decided at the beginning of the game, and both players are informed; the player with this advantage alternates. The same piece does not need to be moved on both moves.

Stalemates and other forms of draws are a thing of the past (for Earthlings) since the game always concludes with a definitive result. If a game ends in a tie, the player last granted the double-move advantages loses. Now, there is no safe way out of a game—a drastic difference from any Earthly rules.

The player promoting a pawn can only turn the pawn into a piece that has already been captured. As a result, a player cannot have two queens or more than two knights, bishops, or rooks on the board simultaneously. According to Tralfamadorians, when pieces are captured, they are not actually gone from the position; they exist in other positions. Or at least, so it goes.

Thrice per game, each player can undo their previous move without wasting their move. Again, the player brings back a previous position because the previous position has happened, is happening, and will always happen.

Once per game, each player can play what is known as a Tralfamadorian Capture. Players can capture any opponent piece that is two moves away; however, this ability is not possible if there is another enemy piece along its path.
